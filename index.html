Physicist turned AI Researcher

I'm a second-year PhD student in the Mehta Family School of Data Science and Artificial Intelligence at IIT Roorkee, where my research focuses on LLM-based agents for automated extraction of material properties and structural features from scientific literature.

My journey began with a deep love for Physics — I qualified IIT JAM (AIR 771) and completed my Masters at IIT Mandi, where I studied Many-Body Localization using Fibonacci quasiperiodic sequences. That rigorous mathematical foundation now drives my AI research.

Beyond research, I co-founded EVA Ad Ventures as CTO, deploying AI models like Real-ESRGAN for 3D anamorphic billboard production. I'm an IndiaAI PhD Fellow, awarded under the Government of India's IndiaAI Mission.

What I Work On

My research sits at the intersection of AI, NLP, and physical sciences — building intelligent systems that understand scientific knowledge.

🤖

Large Language Models & AI Agents

Designing and evaluating LLM-based agentic pipelines for automated reasoning, multi-step information extraction, and tool use in scientific domains.

🔬

AI for Scientific Discovery

Applying NLP and machine learning to accelerate research in materials science — automating extraction of properties, structures, and relationships from literature.

👁️

Multimodal Learning

Vision–language models (BLIP, CLIP) for scientific figure understanding, image captioning, and cross-modal retrieval from research publications.

⚛️

Computational Physics

Numerical studies of quantum many-body systems — Many-Body Localization, level-spacing statistics, and exact diagonalization of disordered lattice models.

🗄️

Research Data Engineering

Building scalable pipelines that collect, parse, and index scientific data (text, tables, figures) into vector databases for semantic search and analysis.

🧪

Materials Informatics

Machine learning on materials science datasets — predictive modeling of material properties, feature engineering, and reducing false positive rates in experimental screening.
